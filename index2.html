Research Experiences

Master of Research (MRes) thesis; Aberystwyth University, UK

I undertook a 1-year research project titled; developing a Novel Diagnostic Method; Multiplex PCR for the diagnosis of Cryptosporidium infection.
This research seeks to comparatively test for diagnostic sensitivities of standard PCR techniques targeting individual loci, and to develop a novel approach that can improve turnaround time and gDNA expenditure by multiplexing primers targeting and amplifying multiple gene loci, which can subsequently be sequenced directly to generate sufficient information for specific genotype and subtype identification in laboratory and clinical samples.

  • Used primer3 bioinformatics tools to design primers for specific regions of Cryptosporidium genome
  • Performed DNA extraction from Cryptosporidium oocyst using freeze-thaw thermal extraction method
  • Performed ligation and cloning of PCR products of interest
  • Performed miniprep purification and qubits assay quantification
  • Performed PCR, RT-PCR and qPCR for gene amplification
  • Perform gel electrophoresis to analyse sizes of separated PCR products
  • Bioinformatics analysis and sequence alignment of genomic data generated by Sanger sequencing of the PCR
    products using NCBI and CryptoDB databases, Biopython, Clustal Omega software.
